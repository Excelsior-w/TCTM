Language-Guided Human Motion Generation Utilizing Cross-Modal Interaction and Textual Enhancement

Li Chaowu*,
DA LIAN University
2025
Paper Code arXiv

Abstract

Text-driven human motion generation aims to generate natural motion sequences with language descriptions. Existing approaches still struggle to accurately align text semantics with motion sequences, and they also have trouble modeling fine-grained motion details effectively. To overcome these obstacles, we introduce a generative framework that combines local and global textual semantics. The proposed framework first employs the Textual Multi-granularity Interpretation Network and the CLIP model to extract text semantic representations at different granularities, thereby comprehensively capturing semantics from fine-grained lexical levels to coarse-grained global meanings. Subsequently, the Cross-modal Dynamic Transformer is introduced to establish dynamic correlations between local text semantics and motion features. Meanwhile, a global self-attention mechanism combined with a masking strategy is applied to enable the modeling of the relationship between global semantics and motion sequences. Our model demonstrates superior performance on major benchmarks, and the results show substantial performance gains in R-Precision and MM-Dist compared to existing techniques. Our work provides new insights into multimodal semantic modeling and natural motion generation.

Methodology

Illustration of our framework, consisting of (a) the Residual Motion VQ-VAE and (b) the Motion Generation stage. The input text description is processed by the TMIN module and the CLIP model to extract local and global text representations, capturing detailed local semantics and overall contextual information, respectively. The local text representations, along with the masked base-layer motion sequence or the unmasked residual motion sequence, are input to the CrossDyT module for dynamic cross-modal fusion modeling. Finally, the predicted base and residual layers are concatenated and passed through the decoder to generate the complete, semantically consistent human motion sequence.
